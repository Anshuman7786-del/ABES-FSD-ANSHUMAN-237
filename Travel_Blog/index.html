Delhi

Delhi, the capital city of India, is a place where history, culture, and modern life come together beautifully. As you wander through Delhi, you’ll find yourself moving between centuries — from ancient Mughal architecture like the Red Fort, Humayun’s Tomb, and the towering Qutub Minar, to the lively streets of Chandni Chowk packed with street food, spice markets, and colorful shops.
If you’re looking for the best places to visit in Delhi, don’t miss the elegance of India Gate, the spiritual calm of the Lotus Temple and Akshardham, or the bustling vibe of Connaught Place. And of course, no Delhi travel experience is complete without tasting its legendary street food — hot parathas, tangy chaats, and sweet jalebis.
Delhi is not just a city, it’s an unforgettable journey. Whether you’re exploring historical monuments, enjoying local cuisine, or simply soaking in the energy of this vibrant metropolis, Delhi will leave you with stories worth sharing.

Things to do
  • Explore Historical Monuments – Visit the Red Fort, Qutub Minar, and Humayun’s Tomb to experience Delhi’s royal past.
  • Taste Delhi Street Food – Try parathas at Paranthe Wali Gali, spicy chaats, momos, and sweet jalebis.
  • Shop at Bustling Markets – Stroll through Chandni Chowk, Sarojini Nagar, and Dilli Haat for clothes, handicrafts, and souvenirs.
  • Find Peace at Temples – Admire the Lotus Temple, Akshardham, and Gurudwara Bangla Sahib for their beauty and serenity.
  • Explore Art & Museums – Discover Indian history and culture at the National Museum, Crafts Museum, or NGMA.

Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, is one of the oldest living cities in the world and the spiritual heart of India. Situated on the banks of the sacred River Ganga, this city is where faith, culture, and tradition blend seamlessly. From witnessing mesmerizing Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat to exploring the labyrinth of narrow lanes filled with temples, silk shops, and chai stalls — Varanasi is an experience that stays with you forever.
Known as the city of temples and ghats, Varanasi is not just a destination, it’s a feeling. Every sunrise over the Ganges and every chant of the mantras adds to its timeless charm. Whether you’re a pilgrim, a traveler, or a seeker of peace, Varanasi welcomes you with open arms and eternal stories.

Things to do
  • Attend the Ganga Aarti – Witness the magical evening ritual at Dashashwamedh Ghat, a spiritual experience like no other.
  • Take a Boat Ride on the Ganges – Especially at sunrise, when the ghats glow with golden light.
  • Visit Famous Temples – Explore Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Sankat Mochan Hanuman Temple, and Durga Temple.
  • Walk Along the Ghats – Assi Ghat, Manikarnika Ghat, and Panchganga Ghat each have unique stories and significance.
  • Sip Banarasi Chai and taste local food – Enjoy it in small clay cups (kulhads) for the true local flavor.Also Relish kachori-sabzi, tamatar chaat, lassi, and the famous Banarasi paan.
  • Shop for Banarasi Silk Sarees – World-famous for their craftsmanship, they make a perfect souvenir.

Jamshedpur

Jamshedpur, often called the Steel City of India, is more than just an industrial hub — it’s a green, well-planned city built by Jamsetji Tata. Surrounded by rolling hills, lakes, and lush forests, Jamshedpur beautifully balances modern industry with natural beauty. It is home to Asia’s first industrial city, yet feels refreshing with its clean roads, parks, and the charm of tribal culture around.
Whether you’re strolling through Jubilee Park, boating at Dimna Lake, or exploring the vibrant markets, Jamshedpur offers a unique mix of relaxation and discovery. For travelers looking to step away from the usual tourist trails, Jamshedpur is a hidden gem in Jharkhand worth exploring.

Things to do
  • Relax at Jubilee Park – A beautifully designed park with fountains, rose gardens, and a zoo, perfect for family outings.
  • Visit Dimna Lake – Surrounded by Dalma Hills, it’s a serene spot for boating, picnics, and photography.
  • Seek Blessings at Bhuvaneshwari Temple – A spiritual site that offers panoramic views of the city.
  • Learn at Russi Modi Centre of Excellence – A fascinating place that showcases Tata’s history, art, and industrial growth.
  • Trek the Dalma Hills – Perfect for adventure seekers who love hiking and nature walks.
  • Taste Local Flavors – Try Jharkhandi dishes and street food like litti-chokha, dhuska, and pua.
